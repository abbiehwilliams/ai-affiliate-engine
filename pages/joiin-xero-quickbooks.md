# How to Combine Xero and QuickBooks Data into One Consolidated Report

Are you running multiple business entities where some use Xero and others use QuickBooks Online? Manually exporting CSVs into Excel every month-end leads to reporting errors, broken formulas, and hours of wasted time.

This guide demonstrates how to automatically consolidate financial statements across Xero and QuickBooks using Joiin.

---

## The Problem
When subsidiaries operate on different accounting systems:
* Account names and Chart of Accounts structures do not match.
* Data must be manually refreshed at every month-end close.
* Multi-currency exchange rates require manual adjustment.

---

## The Solution: Automated Consolidation with Joiin

Joiin acts as a unified reporting layer sitting on top of both Xero and QuickBooks Online.

### Step 1: Connect Both Platforms
1. Log into your [Joiin Dashboard](https://joiin.co/?red=stackm&utm_source=stackm&utm_medium=revshare&utm_affiliate_network=reditus).
2. Go to **Companies** → **Add Company**.
3. Select **Xero** and authorize your Xero entities.
4. Click **Add Company** again, select **QuickBooks Online**, and authorize your QuickBooks entities.

### Step 2: Map Your Chart of Accounts
1. Navigate to **Chart of Accounts**.
2. Group mismatched account codes (e.g., map Xero `400 - Sales` and QuickBooks `4010 - Revenue` under a single group heading: `Group Revenue`).

### Step 3: Run Consolidated Statements
1. Select **Reports** → **Profit & Loss** or **Balance Sheet**.
2. Choose your base reporting currency. Joiin automatically pulls live trial balance data from both Xero and QuickBooks and combines them into one presentation-ready report.
